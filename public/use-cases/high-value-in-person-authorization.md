## What is High-Value In-Person Authorization?

In high-value luxury retail (watches, jewelry, fine art), "Friendly Fraud" is a common problem. A cardholder makes a $15,000 purchase in person, walks out with the goods, and later files a chargeback claiming their card was stolen or they were coerced. Existing digital methods (Chip/PIN) prove the *token* was there, but not the *human's intent*.

A **Verifiable Purchase Token (VPT)** is a "Single-Use Claim" (Criteria §2) generated by the cardholder's banking app for a specific merchant and amount. The merchant scans this claim *during the transaction*. This provides an undeniable, bank-attested record of the customer's physical presence and specific intent, making "It wasn't me" disputes nearly impossible to maintain.

## Data Verified

Issuing bank name, cardholder name (masked), merchant identifier, transaction amount, item description (optional), liveness check success token, timestamp (precise to the second), one-time salt, validity window.

**Document Types:**
- **Verifiable Purchase Token (VPT):** Single-use authorization.
- **Biometric Presence Attestation:** Proof that a liveness check was performed.
- **In-Person Consent Record:** Detailed intent statement for high-value services.

## Data Visible After Verification

Shows the issuer domain (`wellsfargo.com`, `amex.com`) and the token status.

**Status Indications:**
- **Authorized / Valid** — Token is active and ready to be consumed by the merchant.
- **Consumed / Used** — **SUCCESS:** The merchant has scanned and processed this specific authorization.
- **Expired** — **ALERT:** The 60-second window has passed.
- **Revoked** — **ALERT:** The cardholder cancelled the authorization.

## Second-Party Use

The **Cardholder / Customer** benefits from verification.

**Protection Against Forgery:** Ensuring that high-value transactions only occur with their explicit, bank-attested consent.

**Fraud Safety:** If a transaction appears that they *didn't* authorize, the absence of a "Consumed" verified token in their bank history is a strong piece of evidence in their favor.

## Third-Party Use

**Luxury Merchants / POS Operators**
**Chargeback Protection:** By scanning the verified token, the merchant becomes an **Active Witness** to the transaction. They retain the hash and the "Success" result from the bank, which serves as indisputable evidence of the customer's presence and intent during a dispute.

**Banks & Card Issuers**
**Dispute Resolution:** Instantly resolving "Not Me" claims by checking if a verified, liveness-checked token was consumed at the time of purchase.

**Insurers**
**Retail Risk Coverage:** Lowering insurance premiums for luxury merchants who adopt verified in-person authorizations, as it significantly reduces chargeback and identity theft risk.

## Verification Architecture

**The "Indisputable Presence" Fraud Problem**

- **Friendly Fraud:** A cardholder makes a purchase and then claims their phone or card was stolen.
- **Coerced Transactions:** A victim is forced to authorize a payment under duress. The liveness check and app-based authorization provides a "Safe Path" for the bank to detect stress or unusual patterns.
- **Theft Replay:** A thief takes a photo of a QR code or authorization screen to try and use it elsewhere. The context-binding (Merchant ID) and time-locking (60s window) prevent this.

**Issuer Types** (First Party)

**Major Retail Banks.**
**Credit Card Networks.**
**Digital Wallet Providers (Apple/Google Pay integration).**

**Privacy Salt:** Highly critical. Transaction data is extremely sensitive. The hash must be salted and the verification URL should ideally be restricted to authorized merchant POS networks.

## Authority Chain

**Pattern:** Regulated

Barclays Bank, a regulated financial institution, is authorized by the FCA to issue verified high-value in-person transaction authorizations and payment tokens.

```
✓ branch.barclays.co.uk/authorize/verify — Issues verified in-person transaction authorization tokens
  ✓ fca.org.uk/register — Regulates UK financial crime prevention
    ✓ gov.uk/verifiers — UK government root namespace
```

See [Authority Chain Specification](../../docs/authority-chain-spec.md) for the full protocol.

## Rationale

High-value fraud costs the retail industry billions annually. By turning payment authorizations into verifiable digital bridges, we bring "Active Witnessing" to the transaction, making fraud expensive and disputes easy to resolve.

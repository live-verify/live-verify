## What is Breed Pedigree Registration?

A pedigree certificate proves an animal's lineage — who its parents, grandparents, and great-grandparents are, as recorded by an authoritative breed registry. This applies across species:

- **Dogs:** The Kennel Club (UK), AKC (US), FCI (international) — the largest volume by far
- **Horses:** The Jockey Club, Weatherbys, AQHA, breed-specific registries
- **Cattle:** American Angus Association, Hereford Herd Book, Holstein Association
- **Cats:** GCCF, TICA, CFA
- **Other livestock:** Sheep, goats, pigs — breed societies maintain stud books

The certificate is the financial backbone of breeding. A "registered" Golden Retriever puppy sells for 3-5x the price of an unregistered one. A Thoroughbred with a verified pedigree tracing back to champion bloodlines can be worth millions. A Wagyu bull with confirmed genetics commands premium stud fees.

## Data Verified

Registered name, breed, sex, date of birth, microchip/tattoo ID, DNA profile reference, sire and dam (with their registration numbers), extended pedigree (3-5 generations), breeder name, current owner, health test results, championship titles, breeding endorsements or restrictions.

**Document Types:**
- **Registration Certificate:** Proves the animal is recorded in the breed registry.
- **Pedigree Certificate:** Full lineage chart (typically 3-5 generations).
- **Export Pedigree:** For international transfer between registries.
- **Stud Service Certificate:** Confirms a specific mating occurred, signed by both stud and dam owners.
- **Breeding Endorsement:** Registry permission (or restriction) for breeding — some registrations carry a "not for breeding" endorsement.
- **DNA Certificate:** Confirming parentage matches the declared pedigree.

See also: [Livestock Health Certificates](livestock-health-certificates.md) for veterinary/transport verification, [Bloodstock Insurance](bloodstock-insurance.md) for equine financial coverage.

## Data Visible After Verification

Shows the issuer domain (`thekennelclub.org.uk`, `akc.org`, `jockeyclub.com`, `angus.org`) and registration standing.

**Status Indications:**
- **Verified Pedigree** — Lineage confirmed by the breed registry database.
- **Breeding Restricted** — Registration carries a "not for breeding" endorsement. Puppies/foals from this animal cannot be registered.
- **Suspended** — Registration suspended pending investigation (e.g., DNA mismatch, fraud complaint).
- **Cancelled** — Registration revoked. Lineage claims are invalid.
- **Transferred** — Ownership has changed; current owner differs from certificate.

## Second-Party Use

The **Animal Owner / Breeder** benefits from verification.

**Selling puppies/kittens:** Proving to buyers that the advertised "KC-registered" or "AKC-registered" puppy is genuinely registered with the claimed lineage. This is the highest-volume scenario — millions of pedigree pets are sold annually, and buyers routinely receive forged or photocopied papers.

**Stud services:** Proving to a mare/bitch owner that the stallion/stud dog has a verified pedigree and is not under breeding restriction, before committing to a stud fee that can range from hundreds (dogs) to hundreds of thousands (horses).

**Show entry:** Proving registration and breeding eligibility for conformation shows, field trials, and breed-specific competitions.

## Third-Party Use

**Pet Buyers**
**Due Diligence:** The single largest fraud surface. Online puppy scams cost UK consumers an estimated 2 million per year. Buyers are shown pedigree papers (often photographed, not even printed) that are entirely fabricated, belong to a different animal, or are from a puppy farm masquerading as a small breeder. Scanning the certificate with Live Verify instantly reveals whether the registry actually issued it.

**Equine / Livestock Buyers**
**High-Value Purchases:** Verifying lineage before wiring $100,000+ for a horse or bull. A "Grade" horse (unregistered) sold as a "Thoroughbred" for 10x the price is a common fraud pattern.

**Breed Registries (Cross-Registry)**
**Import Verification:** When an animal moves between countries, the destination registry verifies the export pedigree from the origin registry. Currently this involves phone calls and email chains. Live Verify makes it instant.

**Insurance Companies**
**Valuation Basis:** Bloodstock and pet insurance valuations depend on pedigree. An insurer verifying a claim for a "champion-sired" animal can instantly confirm the lineage.

**Kennel / Cattery Inspectors**
**Welfare Audits:** Verifying that a breeding establishment's animals match their claimed registrations, helping detect puppy farms using forged papers.

## Verification Architecture

**The "Pedigree Fraud" Problem**

- **Pedigree Padding:** Fabricating a lineage to sell a "Grade" animal as a pedigree for 3-10x the price. The most common form of animal fraud by volume.
- **Paper Recycling:** Using a deceased animal's genuine pedigree papers for a different, unrelated animal. The microchip won't match, but buyers rarely check before paying.
- **Forged Registration Certificates:** Color-printing fake KC/AKC papers. Surprisingly easy — the certificates are designed for prestige, not security.
- **Breeding Restriction Bypass:** Selling a puppy as "KC-registered, breeding quality" when the parent carries a "not for breeding" endorsement, meaning the offspring cannot be registered.
- **Puppy Farm Laundering:** Industrial breeders forging or recycling pedigree papers to disguise high-volume operations as small hobby breeders.
- **Stud Fee Fraud:** Claiming a dog or stallion has a pedigree or championship record it doesn't actually hold, to charge inflated stud fees.

**Issuer Types** (First Party)

**National Kennel Clubs:** The Kennel Club (UK), AKC (US), CKC (Canada), FCI affiliates.
**Horse Registries:** The Jockey Club, Weatherbys, AQHA, breed-specific societies.
**Cattle/Livestock Breed Societies:** American Angus Association, Hereford Herd Book, Holstein Association.
**Cat Registries:** GCCF, TICA, CFA.
**DNA Testing Laboratories:** (e.g., Embark, Wisdom Panel, Animal Genetics) — for parentage verification certificates.

**Privacy Salt — depends on species:**

**Racehorses and high-value livestock: Salt is CRITICAL.** Horse names, sire/dam combinations, and registration numbers are public knowledge (racing databases, auction catalogues, breed society records). Without a salt, an attacker can hash known horse identities and probe the registry's verification endpoint to map which champion stallions are at which yards — enabling targeted theft of animals worth millions. Because the inputs are guessable, the salt is the only thing preventing enumeration.

To protect against this, racehorse and high-value livestock registries should support **Owner-Initiated Re-Salting with Timeout (OIRST)**: the holder — the registered owner, or a delegate they have authorized (trainer, syndicate manager, solicitor) — logs into the registry portal (or app), requests a time-limited verification for a specific animal, and receives a freshly salted hash with a short TTL (e.g., 24-48 hours). The holder sends this link by email, RCS, or message to the prospective buyer. The buyer verifies within the window; after expiry the endpoint returns 404. This means:
- The holder controls exactly when and to whom verification is available
- There is no permanent hash sitting on a long-lived endpoint for attackers to discover
- Each prospective buyer interaction gets its own ephemeral link — no link accumulation
- The registry can log who requested verification and when, creating an audit trail

See [Technical Concepts: OIRST](../../docs/Technical_Concepts.md#owner-initiated-re-salting-with-timeout-oirst) for the full specification, link format, and comparison with other salt patterns.

**Dogs, cats, and small pets: Salt is unnecessary.** Pet pedigree certificates contain high-entropy data that cannot be guessed: microchip numbers, DNA profile references, and multi-generation registered names (e.g., "CH Doversong Master Craftsman" x "Hartfield Summer Breeze"). Brute-forcing these combinations is impractical even without a salt. The standard persistent verification endpoint is fine — there is no theft incentive comparable to a million-dollar stallion, and no public database of pet microchip numbers to enumerate from.

## Jurisdictional Witnessing (Optional)

Some jurisdictions, contracts, or multi-party workflows may add an independent witness layer. When used, the witnessing firm:

- Receives all hashes from the issuer, and any subsequent changes to the payload as they happen—which may manifest as a new hash, a status change, or even a 404 (record deleted)
- Receives structured content/metadata (key identifiers and dates)
- Does **NOT** receive plaintext or sensitive personal information
- Provides an immutable, timestamped audit trail—available to the jurisdiction on demand, to document holders/third parties during disputes, or as expert witness testimony in legal proceedings

This provides:
- **Non-repudiation:** Issuer cannot deny issuing the document
- **Timestamp proof:** Document existed at a specific time
- **Regulatory audit:** Jurisdictions can inspect the witness ledger for fraud detection
- **Resilience:** Verification works even if issuer's systems go down

**Public Blockchain (Non-Party)**

If a witness layer exists, it may periodically commit rollups to a public blockchain as an additional timestamping mechanism. That is optional, not inherent to the use case. The verification paths would then be:

1. **Issuer domain** — Direct check against the issuer
2. **Witnessing firm** — Independent confirmation with timestamp
3. **Public blockchain** — Decentralized trust anchor via rollup inclusion

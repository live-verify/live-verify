## What is Proof of Childcare Enrolment?

You apply for Tax-Free Childcare. HMRC asks you to confirm your child is enrolled at a registered provider and how many hours per week they attend. You dig through emails to find a letter from the nursery. Or you ask the nursery office to write one. They're busy. It takes a week. You upload a PDF. HMRC's caseworker glances at it. That's the "verification."

The same thing happens when you claim childcare costs through Universal Credit, apply for employer-supported childcare vouchers, request a local authority funded hours place, or need to prove childcare arrangements for a custody dispute. Every time, the proof is a letter or invoice from the provider — a document anyone with a word processor could produce.

Live Verify makes the childcare provider the authoritative source. The provider publishes a salted hash confirming: **"This child is enrolled with us, attending X hours/week, since [date]."** The parent generates a time-limited proof to share with HMRC, DWP, their employer, or a family court. The provider's domain is the trust anchor — not a PDF the parent supplies.

The mandate model: Ofsted-registered childcare providers (or equivalent regulators internationally) are required to let parents generate time-limited, hash-based enrolment confirmations. The provider bears the hosting cost as a condition of registration.

## Data Verified

Childcare provider name, Ofsted registration number (or equivalent), child's full name, child's date of birth, enrolment start date, weekly attendance hours, funded hours allocation.

**Document Types:**
- **Enrolment Confirmation:** Core proof that a child attends a registered provider for a stated number of hours.
- **Funded Hours Confirmation:** Specifically confirming the government-funded hours component (15 or 30 hours in England).
- **Fee Schedule Confirmation:** Confirming the weekly/monthly fee paid, for Tax-Free Childcare or Universal Credit claims.

**Privacy Salt:** Essential. Child's name + nursery name + DOB is low entropy and extremely sensitive. The salt is time-limited (e.g., 30 days) and generated by the parent via the provider's portal. Protects against enumeration of which children attend which nurseries — a safeguarding concern, not just a privacy one.

## Data Visible After Verification

Shows the provider domain (`littleoaks-nursery.co.uk`) and Ofsted registration status.

**Status Indications:**
- **Enrolled** — Child is currently attending, hours as stated.
- **Notice Given** — Parent has given notice; child leaving on [date].
- **Left** — Child no longer enrolled. Last attendance: [date].
- **Suspended** — Enrolment temporarily paused (e.g., family holiday, illness).
- **Hours Changed** — Enrolled but attendance hours have changed since the proof was generated. New proof needed.

## Second-Party Use

The **Parent/Guardian** benefits from verification.

**Tax-Free Childcare Claims:** HMRC requires proof of childcare costs and provider registration. A verifiable claim from the nursery's domain eliminates the PDF-upload-and-wait cycle. The parent generates a fresh proof each quarter (or as needed) and HMRC verifies it in seconds.

**Universal Credit Childcare Element:** DWP requires proof of childcare costs for the childcare element of Universal Credit. Currently one of the most common reasons for payment delays — missing or unclear provider letters. Verifiable proofs would accelerate payment processing significantly.

**Employer-Supported Childcare:** Employers offering childcare benefits or salary sacrifice schemes need proof of enrolment. HR departments can verify instantly rather than filing photocopied letters.

**Custody & Family Court:** In custody disputes, proving that a child is enrolled in nursery (and attending regularly) is often relevant. A verifiable confirmation from the provider's domain is stronger evidence than a letter the parent could have fabricated.

## Third-Party Use

**HMRC**
**Tax-Free Childcare Administration:** Automated hash verification of enrolment claims would reduce manual casework, speed up approvals, and detect fraudulent claims (phantom nurseries, inflated hours) at the point of submission.

**DWP**
**Universal Credit Processing:** The childcare element is one of the most error-prone parts of Universal Credit. Verifiable enrolment proofs would reduce overpayments (parent claims more hours than child attends) and underpayments (processing delays due to missing documentation).

**Local Authorities**
**Funded Hours Allocation:** Councils administer the 15/30 funded hours entitlements. They need to confirm that children are actually enrolled at the claiming provider. Currently relies on headcount returns from providers — which can be inflated. Cross-referencing against hash-based enrolment proofs from the provider's domain adds a verification layer.

**Employers**
**Childcare Benefit Verification:** HR departments administering workplace nurseries, childcare vouchers, or back-up care benefits need proof of external childcare arrangements. Live Verify replaces the annual letter chase.

**Family Courts & Cafcass**
**Welfare Assessments:** Court officers assessing children's welfare in custody proceedings can verify childcare arrangements directly against the provider's domain rather than relying on documents either parent provides.

## The Childcare Proof Fraud Problem

- **Phantom nurseries:** Fabricated providers used to claim Tax-Free Childcare or Universal Credit. The "nursery" doesn't exist — or exists but the child doesn't attend.
- **Hours inflation:** Parent claims 40 hours/week; child attends 15. The extra hours generate fraudulent benefit claims.
- **Forged provider letters:** Letters on fake letterhead confirming enrolment at providers the child doesn't attend.
- **Fee inflation:** Overstating fees to maximise Tax-Free Childcare top-ups or Universal Credit childcare element.
- **Ghost children:** Registering children who don't exist to claim funded hours. Provider and parent may be colluding.

Live Verify stops most of these because the hash resolves against the provider's registered domain. A phantom nursery has no recognised domain. Hours and fees are locked into the hash at the time of proof generation. HMRC/DWP can cross-reference the Ofsted number in the claim against the Ofsted register.

## Verification Architecture

**Issuer Types** (First Party)

**Day Nurseries & Pre-Schools:** Private, voluntary, and independent (PVI) sector providers, registered with Ofsted (England), Care Inspectorate (Scotland), CIW (Wales), or equivalent.
**Childminders:** Registered childminders operating from home.
**Nursery Chains:** Busy Bees, Bright Horizons, N Family Club, Kidsunlimited — large chains with existing digital infrastructure.
**School-Based Nurseries:** Nursery classes within maintained schools or academies.

**Mandate Justification:** Childcare providers are already Ofsted-registered and subject to inspection. Requiring them to offer hash-based enrolment verification is a minimal additional obligation — smaller providers could use a shared platform hosted by a nursery management software provider (e.g., Famly, ParentMail, Tapestry) rather than running their own endpoint.

## Authority Chain

**Pattern:** Institutional (Regulated)

```
✓ littleoaks-nursery.co.uk/enrolment — Ofsted-registered nursery confirming child enrolment
✓ brightfuturesnursery.co.uk/enrolment — Ofsted-registered nursery confirming child enrolment
✓ busybees.com/enrolment — Nursery chain confirming child enrolment
```

Trust derives from Ofsted registration. The Ofsted number in the verification block can be cross-referenced against the public Ofsted register to confirm the provider is genuine and currently registered.

| Country | Regulatory Body | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| **UK (England)** | Ofsted | Tax-Free Childcare, 15/30 funded hours, Universal Credit childcare element |
| **UK (Scotland)** | Care Inspectorate | 1,140 funded hours for 3-5 year olds |
| **USA** | State licensing agencies | Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit, employer-dependent care FSA |
| **Canada** | Provincial regulators | Canada Child Benefit, provincial subsidies vary by province |
| **Australia** | ACECQA + state regulators | Child Care Subsidy (CCS) requires Activity Test and provider confirmation |

| **Germany** | Jugendamt (Youth Welfare Office) | Kita-Gutschein (nursery voucher) system varies by state |
| **Ireland** | Tusla | National Childcare Scheme (NCS) subsidies |

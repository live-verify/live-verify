FOUNDING ADOPTER PROGRAM

The first ten get the author's ear. And this page, permanently.

Every standard has a moment when adopting it stops being brave. Until then, the organizations that move first deserve more than a thank-you — they get the author's advisory attention and a permanent, dated place in the history of the standard.

The deal

  • Who: the first ten pilot issuers (organizations publishing verification endpoints for documents they stand behind) and the first ten SaaS integrations (products that emit verifiable documents for their customers).
  • You get: free advisory support from the standard's author, by email — design review of your canonical text templates, normalization fixtures, endpoint setup, and revocation workflow, up to a sensible advice limit: think a handful of focused exchanges that get your team unstuck, not embedded consulting.
  • The boundary, stated plainly: this is a personal open-source project run in evenings-and-weekends capacity — expect responses within a few days, not hours. If your deployment needs sustained, hands-on implementation work, that's beyond the free scope; I'll say so when we reach it and can refer you to commercial integration partners.
  • We get: permission to name you here and in talks/posts about the standard, and your feedback folded into the spec while it's still cheap to change.
  • Exit anytime: it's an open standard on your own domain. If you stop, you delete files — there is no contract, dependency, or fee at any point.
